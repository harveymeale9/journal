Appendix · Ordinary Matter

Ordinary matter is set thus: justified, in the English hand, opening upon a rubricated versal. Most of the Manual is ordinary matter, and the forms in this appendix are its punctuation — a law here, a table there, a figure where a figure earns its ink.

Cross-references are set in the small capitals of the house, so the eye can follow a corridor without leaving the page: see Entry I, On the Nature of Attention.

Asides, doubts, and instructions to the reader stand in the margin hand — italic, half-voiced, ruled in oxblood — so the main text never has to interrupt itself.

When a page has said enough, it says so, and stops.
